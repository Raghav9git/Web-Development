Day 3 üèûÔ∏è






We woke up early at around 6 am to a fresh and cool morning. After getting ready, we ate puri and dal for breakfast. Then we headed towards Mithun View Gate, which is inside the Kamlang Tiger Reserve, and this was the starting point of our trek for the day.









On the way we met Peter, who is a wildlife photographer and also a guide. From Mithun View Gate, we began our trek at around 9 am. The path went deep into the forest with a lot of uphill walking. It was quite tough but also very scenic, and the whole route felt peaceful and beautiful. The total trek distance was almost 10 km.












After some of trekking, we reached the Kamlang River. To cross it, we walked over a long and high hanging bridge. It moved slightly as we walked, but the view from the top was amazing, and it made the crossing feel exciting.













Soon after the river, we entered the dense part of the forest. Tall bamboo trees‚Äîboth thick and thin varieties‚Äîgrew closely together, along with large banana plants and many types of forest insects. The sunlight barely reached the ground, so it felt much darker than the actual time of day.







When we reached the top of the trek, we came across a small, cute wooden hut that looked warm and welcoming in the middle of the forest. We stopped there to rest and eat our packed lunch. Peter helped us pluck a local yellow fruit called rabab from a nearby tree. The fruit was slightly bitter, but trying it right there made the moment special.









Around 4 pm, we stopped by the bank of the Kamlang River and took a short break, sitting on the large rocks and enjoying the quiet surroundings. After resting for about half an hour, we continued the final stretch of our walk. By around 5:30 pm, we reached Ninashi Resort, where we settled in for the night, tired but satisfied after a long and fulfilling day.

We started the morning calmly and left for Dree Afra riverside campsite in Acheso, Dibang Valley at around 9:30 am. The drive itself was pleasant, and we reached the camp by about 10:45 am.









The campsite turned out to be one of the most beautiful places of our entire trip, set beside a crystal-clear river that looked untouched and absolutely stunning. At around 2:30 pm, we began our cycling trip. Starting from the campsite, we rode nearly 22 km ahead and then cycled back.
