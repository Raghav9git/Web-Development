Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

Does SafeNet replace emergency numbers?
No. SafeNet does not replace emergency numbers such as 112 or local emergency helplines. It works alongside them by providing a real-time, map-based overview of incidents to improve awareness, coordination, and response planning.
When should I use SafeNet?
SafeNet should be used to report incidents, stay aware of nearby risks, and coordinate community support. In life-threatening situations, users should always contact official emergency services first.
Why should authorities and organizations use SafeNet?
Traditional emergency calls handle incidents individually. SafeNet allows authorities, NGOs, and support services to view multiple incidents together on a map, understand patterns, and coordinate resources more effectively.
Who is SafeNet designed for?
SafeNet is designed for citizens, volunteers, NGOs, hospitals, and emergency response organizations. It also helps normal citizens stay informed about their surroundings and make safer decisions.
Is my identity safe when I report an incident?
Yes. SafeNet prioritizes user privacy. Personal identity is not publicly displayed. Only essential incident-related information is shown to ensure safety, awareness, and coordination.
Can I help even if I am not an authority?
Yes. Community members and volunteers can offer help, share information, and respond to requests without needing to be an official authority.
Does SafeNet work in multiple languages?
Yes. SafeNet is designed to support multiple local languages so people from different regions can use it easily and effectively.
What does each section of the SafeNet website do?
Report allows users to submit incidents. View Incidents shows reported incidents on a map and in a list. Analytics provides basic insights and trends. Your Safety offers safety guidance. Want To Help and Community Response enable collaboration. Services is used by organizations to monitor and coordinate actions.
